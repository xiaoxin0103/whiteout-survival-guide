Optimizing your Bear Hunt damage compounds into every other aspect of account progression. This guide consolidates the latest 2026 meta — covering rally captain setups through Gen 15, Cyrille's expert talent tree, joiners, formations, and the critical F2P-versus-P2W divide.

## Rally Captain Meta — The Core of Your Damage

Your rally captain setup is the single biggest lever on your Bear Hunt score. Three heroes march as captain, and their expedition skills, star levels, and synergy determine your baseline damage before joiners and formation multipliers even apply.

### P2W Captain Setup — The Eternal Trio

| Slot | Hero | Generation | Role | Why They Are Irreplaceable |
|:---:|---|---|---|---|
| **Slot 1 (Tank)** | **Jeronimo** | Gen 1 | Infantry / Buffer | +25% troop damage dealt (expedition skill, percentage-based — scales forever). Also provides +15% global troop ATK buff to your entire account. Undisputed #1 captain tank across ALL generations through Gen 15. |
| **Slot 2 (Lancer)** | **Mia** | Gen 3 | Lancer / AoE DPS | 378% AoE damage + stun on a Lucky Wheel hero. Her raw damage numbers still compete at Gen 15. No lancer has ever dethroned her for Bear Hunt. |
| **Slot 3 (Marksman)** | **Variable by Gen** | Gen 7–15 | Marksman / DPS | Rotates with each generation. Best available marksman fills this slot. See the generation table below. |

**The P2W formula:** `Jeronimo + Mia + (best available marksman)` has been the optimal Bear Hunt captain setup from Gen 3 through Gen 15. Jeronimo is a paid hero (VIP package only), which is why this configuration is P2W-locked. No amount of Lucky Wheel luck or shard grinding will unlock him without spending.

## Joining Heroes — The 4-Slot Rule

When you JOIN someone else's Bear Hunt rally (not captain), a completely different set of rules applies. Only your **first hero's first expedition skill** is evaluated. The rally system scans all joiners and selects the **4 highest-level expedition skills** to apply as global buffs to the entire rally.

### S-Tier Joiner Heroes

| Hero | Rarity | Expedition Skill (Max) | Effective Bonus | Availability |
|------|:---:|---|---|---|
| **Jessie** | Epic (Purple) | Double Shot — +25% troop damage dealt | +25% damage for all rally participants | Extremely common; easy to max |
| **Jasser** | Epic (Purple) | Frost Arrow — +25% troop damage dealt | +25% damage for all rally participants | Extremely common; easy to max |
| **Jeronimo** | Mythic (Paid) | Commander's Wrath — +25% troop damage dealt | +25% damage for all rally participants | P2W only; maxes at level 5 like the epics |
| **Seo-yoon** | Epic (Purple) | Flame Strike — +25% troop damage dealt | +25% damage for all rally participants | Common; easy to max |

All four provide the exact same +25% troop damage boost when maxed to level 5. The difference is accessibility: Jessie and Jasser are purple (epic) heroes that every player maxes naturally through normal gameplay. Jeronimo provides the same buff but costs real money.

### The 4-Slot Rule in Practice

```
Scenario: 7-player rally (1 captain + 6 joiners)

If 4 joiners each send a Level-5 Jessie in their first slot:
→ Rally receives +25% × 4 = +100% troop damage bonus
→ The 5th and 6th joiners' first-slot skills are IGNORED
→ This is the maximum possible joiner bonus

If 3 joiners send Level-5 Jessie and 3 send random mythic heroes:
→ Only 3 buffs are applied: +75% troop damage
→ The random mythic skills (if not maxed) are wasted slots
```

### Joiner Priority and Rules

| Rule | Explanation | Consequence |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Always send a maxed purple hero first** | Jessie, Jasser, or Seo-yoon at skill level 5 | Guaranteed +25% contribution |
| **Never send mythic heroes as joiner** | Unless you are 100% certain their expedition skill is level 5 | A level-1 Jeronimo is WORSE than a level-5 Jessie — and may overwrite someone else's maxed buff |
| **If your joiner is already marching** | Send a blue (rare) or green hero in your first slot | Prevents your lower-level skill from overwriting a maxed Jessie/Jasser buff already in the rally |
| **4 is the hard cap** | The system takes the top 4 skills only; 5th and 6th joiners contribute nothing via skills | Beyond 4 joiners, only troop count matters |
| **Skill level > hero rarity** | A level-5 purple hero beats a level-1 mythic hero every time | Do not be fooled by rarity |

Instant march time means more rallies joined per 30-minute event window — and more damage equals more rewards. ## Troop Formation — The 1/1/8 Standard and Beyond

Bear Hunt is a pure DPS race. The bear does not fight back (no tanking required), has no positional mechanics (no flanking benefit), and sits stationary for the full duration. This makes **Marksmen** — the highest single-target damage troop type — the undisputed optimal choice.

### Formation Evolution by Generation

| Formation | Ratio (Inf/Lanc/Marks) | Marksman % | Relative Damage | Minimum Gen |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Balanced | 3/3/4 | 40% | 100% (baseline) | Gen 1 |
| Marksman-light | 2/3/5 | 50% | 130% | Gen 2–3 |
| Semi-heavy | 1/2/7 | 70% | 175% | Gen 4–6 |
| **Standard heavy** | **1/1/8** | **80%** | **210%** | **Gen 7+** |
| Extreme | 1/1/98 | 98% | ~220% | Gen 9+ |

### The 1/1/8 Ratio Explained

| Barracks Type | Percentage | Purpose in Bear Hunt |
|:---:|:---:|---|
| **Infantry** | 10% | Bare minimum — only included because the game requires at least one infantry slot. Contributes negligible damage. |
| **Lancer** | 10% | Minimal — lancers have decent ATK but their flanking mechanic is wasted on a stationary bear. Included for formation bonuses. |
| **Marksman** | 80% | The workhorse — highest base ATK, longest range, zero chance of being targeted. Every percentage point you can shift here increases total damage. |

### Gen 9+ Extreme Formation: 1/1/98

Starting at Gen 9, some top-ranking players push the formation to an extreme **1/1/98** ratio — that is, 1% infantry, 1% lancer, and 98% marksmen. This is achieved by training disproportionately more marksmen and carefully managing your barracks ratios.

| Formation | Infantry | Lancer | Marksman | Damage Index | Risk |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1/1/8 | 10% | 10% | 80% | 210% | None — safe for all content |
| 1/1/98 | 1% | 1% | 98% | ~220% | Slightly weaker in PvP defense if attacked during Bear Hunt |

> **Recommendation:** The 1/1/8 formation is sufficient for 99% of players. The 1/1/98 extreme setup gains a marginal ~5% damage increase with significantly higher resource and training costs. Only pursue it if you are a dedicated Bear Hunt specialist and your alliance covers your PvP defense.

### Why Not 0/0/100?

The game requires at least one hero from each troop type to form a valid march. Since your captain lineup uses Infantry (Jeronimo/Hector), Lancer (Mia), and Marksman slots, you must bring at least some troops of each type.

### Cyrille Talent Tree — Complete Priority Order

| Priority | Talent Name | Max Effect | What It Does | F2P Target |
|:---:|---|---|---|---|
| 🥇 #1 | **Earthbreaker** | +30% personal Bear Hunt damage | Multiplicative damage multiplier — applied AFTER all other bonuses. A 10M-damage rally becomes 13M. | Max immediately |
| 🥈 #2 | **Weapon Master** | +5 Essence Stones per Bear Hunt | Grants bonus Essence Stones after each hunt. Essence Stones are the rarest hero gear upgrade material — this talent pays for itself within 2-3 weeks. | Max after Earthbreaker |
| 🥉 #3 | **Scavenging** | +20% hero gear Enhancement XP | All Enhancement XP earned from Bear Hunt is increased by 20%. Accelerates gear progression across your entire hero roster. | Max after Weapon Master |
| 4 | **Rally Capacity** | +15% troops per Bear Hunt rally | Increases the number of troops you can bring as captain. More troops = more damage. | Max after core three |

### Cyrille Investment Roadmap

```
Phase 1: Unlock Cyrille → Put ALL points into Earthbreaker until +30%
Phase 2: Max Weapon Master → +5 guaranteed Essence Stones per hunt
Phase 3: Max Scavenging → +20% Enhancement XP from Bear Hunt
Phase 4: Max Rally Capacity → +15% troop count as captain
Phase 5: Remaining points → March Speed, then Essence Stone Drop Rate
```

### Why Earthbreaker is Number One

Earthbreaker is a **final-stage multiplicative multiplier**, not an additive bonus. Here is the math:

```
Without Earthbreaker:
  Base ATK × (1 + Hero Bonuses) × (1 + Joiner Bonuses) × (1 + Research + Gear) × Formation Multiplier
  = 1.0 × 1.5 × 2.0 × 1.8 × 0.8
  = 4.32x damage multiplier

With +30% Earthbreaker:
  4.32 × 1.30
  = 5.62x damage multiplier

Net gain: +30% final damage (not +30% of one additive component)
```

If your rally deals 8,000,000 damage without Earthbreaker, it will deal approximately 10,400,000 with it — a gain of 2.4 million per rally. Over 6 rallies in one Bear Hunt event, that is an extra **14.4 million damage** from this single talent.

### Cyrille Synergies

| Combo | Components | Effect |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Earthbreaker + Jeronimo** | +30% (Cyrille) × +25% (Jeronimo) | Multiplicative stack — 1.30 × 1.25 = 1.625x combined personal multiplier |
| **Earthbreaker + 4× Jessie joiners** | +30% (Cyrille) × (1 + 1.00 joiner bonus) | 1.30 × 2.00 = 2.60x damage on the rally's base |
| **Full Bear Hunt Stack** | Cyrille + Jeronimo + 4× Jessie + Trap Level 5 | Maximum theoretical damage ceiling for any individual captain |

## Damage Calculation — How It All Comes Together

Understanding the damage formula helps you prioritize where to invest. Here is the approximate Bear Hunt damage model at Gen 15:

```
Total Bear Hunt Damage =
    Base Troop ATK
    × (1 + Hero ATK Bonuses)              ← Hero skills, gear, widgets
    × (1 + Rally Joiner Bonuses)          ← Up to +100% from 4 maxed joiners
    × (1 + Cyrille Earthbreaker)          ← +30% multiplicative
    × (1 + Estrella Debuff)               ← +15% increased damage taken (Gen 15+)
    × (1 + Research Bonuses)              ← Battle research tree
    × (1 + Hero Gear Bonuses)             ← Gear sets, exclusive weapons
    × (1 + Alliance Tech + Trap Level)    ← Alliance contributions
    × Formation Multiplier                ← 0.80 for 1/1/8 (80% marksmen)
```

### Damage Component Priority — Where to Invest

| Component | Max Potential | F2P Accessibility | Investment Priority |
|-----------|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Joiner Bonuses** | +100% (4 × +25%) | ✅ Free — just coordinate with your alliance | 🥇 #1 (free damage) |
| **Cyrille Earthbreaker** | +30% | ✅ Free — expert talent points from events | 🥇 #1 (free multiplier) |
| **Hero ATK Bonuses** | Varies by hero and stars | 🟡 Requires hero investment | 🥈 #2 (long-term grind) |
| **Research Bonuses** | ~50-80% cumulative | 🟡 Requires speedups and time | 🥈 #2 (steady progress) |
| **Alliance Tech + Trap** | +25% (trap level 5) | ✅ Free — alliance-wide | 🥉 #3 (alliance-dependent) |
| **Hero Gear** | Varies significantly | 🔴 Expensive (Essence Stones bottleneck) | 🥉 #3 (slow grind) |
| **Formation** | 80% marksman ratio | ✅ Free — just train troops | 🥇 #1 (free, immediate) |

### The 80/20 Rule of Bear Hunt Damage

Roughly 80% of your Bear Hunt damage comes from four things that are **completely free** or extremely low-cost:

1. **Correct formation** (1/1/8) — just train the right troops
2. **Cyrille's Earthbreaker** — grind expert events
3. **4 coordinated joiners** — talk to your alliance
4. **Joining many rallies** — teleport to ring 3 and be active

The remaining 20% comes from hero stars, gear, widgets, research, and alliance technology — the pay-to-accelerate components.
