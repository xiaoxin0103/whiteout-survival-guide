# 🐻 Bear Hunt Complete Guide — Maximize Your Damage

> **Bear Hunt is the most important PvE mode in Whiteout Survival.** Your damage = your alliance's rewards. This guide covers everything from beginner basics to Gen 7+ meta troop ratios.

---

## What Is Bear Hunt?

Bear Hunt (officially "Bear Trap" or "Polar Terror") is an alliance boss event where every member attacks a shared bear target. Total alliance damage determines rewards for everyone.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Event Type** | Alliance PvE boss |
| **Frequency** | Multiple times per week (alliance-leader triggered) |
| **Key Metric** | Total damage per attack (not kills) |
| **Rewards** | Hero shards, gems, speedups, alliance credits |

---

## The Basic Formula

Your Bear Hunt damage depends on **three factors**, in order of importance:

```
1. Hero Composition (60%)  ← Heroes, levels, skills
2. Troop Count & Ratio (25%) ← How many troops, what type
3. Buffs & Tech (15%)     ← Research, gear, alliance tech
```

Get #1 and #2 right before worrying about #3.

## The Troop Ratio: 1/1/8 Meta (Gen 7+)

### What It Means

```
Infantry : Lancer : Marksman
    1    :    1   :    8
```

**80% of your troops should be Marksmen.** Your Infantry and Lancers exist to protect the Marksmen and debuff the bear. The Marksmen do the damage.

### How to Set It Up

1. Open your **March Formation** before attacking
2. Set **Infantry to exactly 10%** of your march capacity
3. Set **Lancers to exactly 10%**
4. Set **Marksmen to exactly 80%**
5. **Do NOT auto-fill.** Auto-fill uses balanced ratios which cuts your damage by 40-60%.

### Why 1/1/8?

Bear attacks are single-target and relatively slow. You only need enough Infantry to absorb the first few hits (before your heroes' skills kick in). Lancers are there for the debuff, not damage. Marksmen have the highest single-target DPS in the game — let them carry.

## Step-by-Step Attack Sequence

### Before the Attack

- [ ] Heal ALL troops (check hospital)
- [ ] Set troop ratio to 1/1/8
- [ ] Activate **Attack Boost** (if available)
- [ ] Activate **Pet Buff** (if you have the Bear pet)
- [ ] Check your heroes are equipped with best gear

### During the Attack

1. **Start the rally or solo attack**
2. Watch hero skill rotations — some heroes (like Jeronimo) have timing-dependent buffs
3. After the attack, **immediately heal** and prepare for the next round
4. Alliance leaders: stagger your strongest attackers to avoid overkill on early rounds

## Bear Hunt Damage Formula (Advanced)

```
Total Damage = (Marksman ATK × 0.8 + Lancer ATK × 0.15 + Infantry ATK × 0.05)
               × Hero Skill Multiplier
               × March Capacity
               × (1 + Damage Buffs)
               × (1 - Bear Defense Reduction)
```

### What This Means in Practice

| Factor | How to Increase |
|--------|----------------|
| **Marksman ATK** | Level up marksman heroes, upgrade marksman gear |
| **Hero Skill Multiplier** | Level hero skills, unlock Exclusive Weapons |
| **March Capacity** | Research, HQ level, VIP level |
| **Damage Buffs** | Attack boost items, alliance tech, hero buff skills |
| **Bear Defense Reduction** | Debuff heroes (Flint, Gregory), armor-piercing research |

## Common Bear Hunt Mistakes

| Mistake | Impact | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| **Auto-fill troops** | -40% to -60% damage | Manually set 1/1/8 ratio |
| **Wrong hero order** | Buffs don't stack correctly | Place debuffers first, DPS last |
| **Attacking with wounded troops** | Reduced march capacity | Always heal before attacking |
| **Ignoring Exclusive Weapons** | Missing 30-50% damage potential | Push carry hero's weapon to Level 20 |
| **Using balanced hero comp** | Suboptimal damage | Focus on marksmen-heavy comp |
